Who is SZA

A Generation's Voice

Born Solána Imani Rowe in Maplewood, New Jersey, SZA redefined what R&B could be. Equal parts vulnerable and fierce, her music pulls from personal heartbreak, spiritual searching, and an unapologetic femininity that resonates across generations.

From the raw intimacy of CTRL to the sweeping ambition of SOS — the longest-running #1 album by a female artist in Billboard 200 history — she has cemented her place as one of the defining voices of her era.

Biography

An Artist Unfiltered

Born November 8, 1989, in Maplewood, New Jersey, SZA grew up between the suburbs and the soul of New York City. Raised in a Muslim household with a diverse musical upbringing, she developed an intimate relationship with sound long before she ever stepped into a studio.

Her artistry defies simple categorization — weaving R&B, soul, indie, and hip-hop into something undeniably her own. From her early EPs to the critically acclaimed CTRL and SOS, SZA has built one of the most resonant catalogs in contemporary music.

A former gymnastics competitor and marine biology student, her intellectual curiosity and emotional depth permeate every lyric she writes. She signed to Top Dawg Entertainment in 2013 — the label's first female signee.
